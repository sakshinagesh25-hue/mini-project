Chocolet Cake

Ingedients:

  1. 1.5 cups All-purpose flour (Maida)
  2. 1 cup Powdered sugar
  3. 1/4 cup Unsweetened cocoa powder
  4. 1 cup Milk (at room temperature)
  5. 1/2 cup Vegetable oil
  6. 1 teaspoon Baking powder
  7. 1/2 teaspoon Baking soda
  8. 1 teaspoon Vanilla essence

Step-by-Step Instructions:

    1. First, preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and grease a baking tin with oil and a dusting of flour.

    2. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and baking soda to remove any lumps.

    3. In another bowl, whisk the milk, oil, and sugar until the sugar is completely dissolved

    4. Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture and add vanilla essence. Whisk gently until you get a smooth batter.

    5. Pour the batter into the prepared tin and bake for 30 to 35 minutes.

    6. Insert a toothpick into the center; if it comes out clean, the cake is ready.

    7. Allow the cake to cool completely before removing it from the tin and serving.

      ⚠️ Worning & Precaution

    1. ⚠️ Don’t Overfill Pans: Fill the baking pan no more than 2/3 to 3/4 full to avoid overflowing.

    2. ⚠️ Preheat the Oven: Always preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) for at least 15 minutes before baking.

    3. ⚠️ Check for Freshness: Ensure your baking soda and baking powder are not expired, or the cake won't rise.

    4. ⚠️ Use Proper Temperature: Use an oven thermometer to ensure the temperature is accurate. A too-hot oven creates cracked tops, while a too-low oven causes the cake to collapse.

    5. ⚠️ Don't Open the Oven Early: Avoid opening the oven door for at least the first 20 minutes to prevent the cake from sinking in the middle.

    6. ⚠️ Test for Doneness: Insert a toothpick or skewer into the center; if it comes out clean, the cake is done. If not, bake for a few minutes more.

    7. ⚠️ Cooling Time: Let the cake cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to prevent it from breaking.
